I am a Master's student in [Neuroscience at the University of Freiburg](https://uni-freiburg.de/studium/studienangebot/studiengang/685/), Germany, and a [DAAD](https://www.daad.de/de/) Scholarship recipient. I am also enrolled in the Master's program in Computational Neuroscience at Kyoto University, currently on leave after completing the first semester.

My academic background is in Electrical and Electronic Engineering, having earned a B.Eng. from Kyoto University. During my undergraduate studies, I became interested in how humans learn from both direct experience and observation, and how these processes can be described using computational models.

My research interests lie at the intersection of computational neuroscience, cognitive science, and neurotechnology. In particular, I am interested in learning and decision-making, reinforcement learning, inverse reinforcement learning, individual differences, and the neural mechanisms underlying human cognition and behavior.

My undergraduate research focused on computational modeling of observational learning and self-learning in decision-making. Using behavioral experiments, reinforcement learning, and inverse reinforcement learning, I investigated how individuals integrate different sources of information during learning and how cognitive traits vary across individuals.

Currently, I am working as a Research Assistant (HiWi) in the [Neuropsychology Lab at the University of Freiburg](https://uni-freiburg.de/neuropsychologie/), where I contribute to research involving sleep, memory, EEG, and physiological signal analysis.

In the long term, I hope to contribute to human-centered computational neuroscience and neurotechnology by integrating behavioral experiments, computational modeling, neuroimaging, and neural signal analysis.
